# Instructions: How to Restring a Concert Xylophone with a Broken Cord

<h3>Removing the Old Cord</h3>
<img width="400" height="300" alt="Image of Bungee Cord" src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/61sVT1SB8fL._AC_SX679_.jpg"> <br>
1. Get your supplies: a pair of scissors, a paper clip, a lighter, and around 50 feet of 1/8" bungee cord. The amount of cord you need will vary based on how many keys your xylophone has and the width of each key, but generally a concert xylophone has ~42 keys each 1-1.5 inches in width, so that would be ~25 feet of cord. I usually opt for extra. <br>
[Source: https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0C5JNGKMH/ref=sspa_dk_detail_6] <br>

<img width="400" height="300" alt="Image of a Concert Xylophone" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/11756c76-7aba-4578-830e-e69a64452681" /> <br>
2. Walking around your xylophone, locate which side has been previously tied. This side will typically have small metal springs that can be easily rolled off. <br>
[Source: https://music.colostate.edu/percussion-ensembles/adams-concert-xylophone/] <br>

3. Cut the cord on this side near the tie. <br>
4. Go to the opposite end of the xylophone. This side should look like a piece of rope looped into two small metal holes. <br>
5. Begin to pull the cord out from this side. This will likely take a while. If the string gets caught on a key, pick up the key and pull out the string straight from that hole before returning to the side. <br>
6. Now that the broken string has been removed, ensure all keys are still placed in their original positions to make restringing easier, return to the side the tie originally was. <br>

<h3>Preparing the Cord</h3>
7. Flatten the paper clip out into a small metal rod, ensuring it's longer than the width of each key. <br>
<img width="400" height="300" alt="An Aberdeen Fish Hook" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/83401551-430f-4b31-9dbc-f1c9e57ab08a"/> <br>
8. Form half of the paper clip into a small aberdeen fish hook like noted above. It has to be thin enough to fit in the small holes on the keys and the metal loops on the instrument. <br>
[Source: https://gamakatsu.com/product/aberdeen-hooks/] <br>
9. Burn the loose end of the bungee cord to fuse the strings together. <br>
10. Hook the burnt end on the bungee cord through your paperclip hook. You should now have a relatively sturdy thredding device. <br>

<h3>Restringing</h3>
11. On the end you originally had the knot, slightly lift the key closest to you. <br>
12. Thread the bungee cord through the top small metal loop on the bar. <br>
13. Thread the bungee cord through the hole found on the top half of the key. <br>
14. Thread the bungee cord through the next metal loop before placing down the bar. <br>
15. Repeat steps 11-14 for every bar until you reach the end of the instrument. <br>
16. Once at the end, repeat steps 11-15, this time on the end you had the loop and going through the bottom loop and hole on the lower half of the keys. <br>
17. When you get back to the end you originally had tied, tightly tie the hooked end of the bungee cord to the string sticking out of the first key strung. <br>
18. If your tie originally had metal springs on it, place those back on. <br>
17. Repeat steps 9-18 for the bottom row of keys. <br>
